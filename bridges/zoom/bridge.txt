ZOOM BRIDGE (phase7)
====================

A bot that joins a Zoom meeting and bridges its audio/video/chat/screen-share
into a LiveKit room, where the teaching brain (apps/agent-runtime) is already
running. The brain is written once; this bridge only moves media.

CONTRACT
- Join via Zoom Meeting SDK / raw media; publish remote participants' audio and
  active screen-share into the LiveKit room.
- Subscribe to the agent's published audio + screen-share and render back into
  Zoom.
- Surface recording + data-retention disclosures in the meeting.

STATUS
- IMPLEMENTED + TESTED (platform-agnostic): the bridge engine lives in
  aoep_shared.bridges (meeting-URL parsing, Zoom Meeting SDK signature minting,
  the join->bridge->leave lifecycle, bidirectional track wiring, chat->Tutor
  routing, and recording/retention disclosures). Wired into the integrations
  service at GET /bridges, POST /bridges/zoom/connect,
  POST /bridges/sessions/{id}/chat|disconnect. Exercised end-to-end via an
  in-memory transport (packages/shared tests + integrations API tests, and the
  `simulate` flag on the connect endpoint).
- REQUIRES TO GO LIVE: the Zoom Linux Meeting SDK native binding (the actual
  audio/video transport) plus ZOOM_SDK_KEY / ZOOM_SDK_SECRET. The binding is not
  bundled in this environment, so a real connect fails closed with
  BridgeUnavailable until it is installed and a ZoomMeetingTransport is wired in
  behind aoep_shared.bridges.session.MediaTransport.
