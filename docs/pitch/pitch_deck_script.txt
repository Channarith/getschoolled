SLIDE 1 — THE STORY (Introductory Hook)
"The Classroom of 1825"
==============================================================

SLIDE CONTENT
  One teacher.
  One pace.
  Thirty different minds.

  200 years. Nothing changed.

NARRATION
  In 1825, a classroom looked exactly like this.
  One teacher at the front. One pace set for everyone.
  And thirty children — each one learning at a completely different
  speed — trying to fit into a single mould.

  The fastest learner: bored. Ahead of the class, mentally elsewhere.
  The middle learner: barely keeping up. Surviving, not thriving.
  The slowest learner: quietly, invisibly, being left behind.

  That was 1825. It is still happening today — in every school,
  in every country, in every language on earth.

  For two centuries, we have asked human teachers to do the impossible:
  know every student personally, teach at thirty different speeds
  simultaneously, remember every gap, every strength, every language.

  No one can do that. But AI can.

SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM
"Education Is Broken in Three Directions"
==============================================================

SLIDE CONTENT
  THE PROBLEM

  1. Personalization gap: One pace fails 66% of students
  2. Access gap: 1 in 5 children globally have no quality teacher
  3. Skills gap: 85M jobs will go unfilled by 2030 due to
     wrong-skill education

  The status quo costs humanity $89 trillion in lost productivity
  every decade.

NARRATION
  The problem is not one thing — it is three compounding failures.

  The personalization gap. In a classroom of thirty, only the
  student learning at exactly the class pace gets optimal instruction.
  The other twenty-nine — two-thirds — are getting suboptimal
  teaching every single day.

  The access gap. One in five children worldwide does not have access
  to a qualified teacher for their subject. In sub-Saharan Africa,
  that number rises to one in two. High-quality, patient, one-on-one
  instruction is a privilege of the wealthy — nowhere else.

  The skills gap. The World Economic Forum estimates that 85 million
  jobs will go unfilled by 2030 because the world is teaching people
  the wrong things. Traditional education moves too slowly to track
  the economy. The result: youth unemployment coexists with desperate
  employer demand.

  Together these three gaps cost an estimated $89 trillion in lost
  human productivity over the next decade. That is not a problem.
  That is the largest unsolved market on earth.

VIDEO DIRECTION
  Three animated split-screen panels, one per problem. Each with a
  data callout in large type. Clean, dark, minimal. Cut to world map
  showing teacher shortage heat map. Duration: 25 seconds.

  What makes Salareen different is not one feature. It is an

  First: our AI is honest. Every answer shows its sources. Every

  never hallucinates an answer and passes it off as fact. In

  capable of handling five million learners a day — runs on the

  outcomes, skill gaps, and course recommendations with a single

  and is already deployed in production.

  And finally: the same teaching brain that runs in a browser

  for the embodied AI future from day one.

SLIDE 7 — BUSINESS MODEL
"Three Revenue Streams. One Platform."
==============================================================

SLIDE CONTENT
  HOW WE MAKE MONEY

  B2C SUBSCRIPTIONS (consumer learners)
    Free tier    — limited classes, one language, basic AI
    Pro tier     — $15/month — unlimited classes, 5 languages, memory
    Premium tier — $29/month — all 27 languages, 1:1 AI tutor,
                    Drive Mode, recordings, advanced analytics

  B2B SEAT LICENSING (corporate + institutional)
    Per-seat     — $15-30/seat/month (volume discounts)
    Site license — flat annual fee for unlimited internal seats
    API license  — platform-as-a-service for other EdTech builders

  MARKETPLACE + ADD-ONS
    Curriculum marketplace — creators earn 70%, platform earns 30%
    Proctored certifications — $49-199 per exam
    Premium personalization — add-on per learner
    Metered GPU minutes — pay-as-you-go for heavy AI use

  UNIT ECONOMICS (at scale)
    Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):  ~$8 per B2C user (digital)
    Lifetime Value (LTV B2C Pro):     ~$252 (18-month avg retention x $15/mo)
    LTV/CAC ratio:                    31.5x
    Gross margin:                     ~72% at Pro tier scale
    Payback period:                   < 1 month at Pro tier

NARRATION
  Our business model is a classic three-tier structure built for
  both virality and enterprise.

  On the consumer side, the free tier is the top of the funnel.
  Learners join free, experience the AI tutor, and convert to Pro
  at $15 per month for the full personalized experience.
  Premium at $29 unlocks the full 27-language platform with 1:1
  tutoring and Drive Mode — targeting the learner who is serious
  about career advancement.

  On the B2B side, we license per seat to corporations running
  internal learning and development programs. At $15 to $30 per
  seat per month — well below the $45-80 per month average for
  corporate LMS platforms — we have a compelling cost advantage
  while delivering AI personalization they do not have anywhere else.

  The marketplace model lets curriculum creators publish and earn
  a 70% revenue share, which builds supply without building cost.
  Proctored certifications at $49 to $199 add a high-margin
  product line that employers actively request.

  The unit economics are exceptional. Our estimated customer
  acquisition cost of eight dollars against an 18-month average
  LTV of $252 at the Pro tier gives us an LTV-to-CAC ratio of
  31.5 to one. And because the marginal cost of one more learner
  is $0.0012 per month, our gross margins reach 72% at Pro scale.

  but it is confined to China, teaches Chinese, and requires

  We are not trying to out-Duolingo Duolingo. We are building
  the platform that makes the category question obsolete. When a
  learner asks "where do I go to actually learn something?" the

==============================================================
SPEAKER TIPS
==============================================================

- Start with the story (Unteachable Wisdom video or classroom-of-1825
  narrative). Do not lead with a company name or funding ask.
- Pause after "What if every learner had their own teacher?" — let it
  land. It is the thesis of the company in one sentence.
- The competitive slide is the most important trust-building moment.
  Acknowledge competitors genuinely. Investors know if you're bluffing.
- On the $0.0012 cost figure: say it slowly, spell it out. It will
  not be believed unless you explain it is measured on the actual
  production stack.
- Close by returning to the opening story. If you began with a
  child being left behind in 1825, end by naming what their life
  looks like with Salareen. Make it human, not a number.
- The ask is the last thing you say, not the first. Investors fund
  missions, then they evaluate returns. Give them the mission first.
