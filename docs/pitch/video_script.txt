SALAREEN — PITCH VIDEO NARRATION SCRIPT
Full voiceover script for the investor pitch video
Integrates with: "Salareen Unteachable Wisdom" (opening),
and docs/demos/salareen_investor_pitch.mp4 (supporting visuals)
Version 0.10.0 | June 2026
==============================================================

TOTAL RUNTIME TARGET: 4 minutes 30 seconds (self-running deck)
VOICE TONE: Warm. Measured. Confident. Not a sales pitch — a story.
KEY RULE: Never rush. Let the images breathe.

==============================================================
SEGMENT 1 — OPENING STORY [0:00 - 0:45]
Visual: The Classroom of 1825
Music: Soft, minimalist piano. Single instrument. Slow tempo.
==============================================================

[PAUSE — 3 seconds of silence over the image of an old classroom]

There is a room you have been in before.

[The image slowly reveals: rows of desks, a single teacher at the
front, thirty children of different abilities]

One teacher.
One pace.
Thirty different minds.

[Three children are highlighted in turn]

That child in the back row — the one staring out the window?
She understood this lesson twenty minutes ago. She is bored.
She will never reach her potential in this room.

That child in the middle? He is keeping up — barely.
He will not ask for help. He does not want to seem slow.
He will graduate knowing less than he should.

And the child in the front left — the one who is quietly falling
behind, too proud to raise her hand, too far behind to catch up?
She will leave school believing she is not smart.

She is wrong. But nobody told her.

This classroom was built in 1825.

[Long pause. Single statistic appears on screen]

It has not changed.

==============================================================
SEGMENT 2 — THE QUESTION [0:45 - 1:00]
Visual: Black screen. White text. Single question.
Music: Music stops. Silence.
==============================================================

[Silence for two full seconds]

What if every learner had their own teacher?

[Hold. Three seconds.]

Not a worksheet.
Not a chatbot.
Not a video that plays at the same speed for everyone.

A teacher.
Patient. Adaptive. In any language.
One that remembers every student. Forever.

==============================================================
SEGMENT 3 — THE PRODUCT [1:00 - 2:15]
Visual: Product demos cycling — Live class, Drive Mode, Languages,
        Careers, Kids Mode
Music: Subtle, forward-moving background rhythm — not distracting
==============================================================

That is Salareen.

[The Bayon Buddy mascot appears, then transitions to the platform UI]

One AI campus. Thousands of classes.

[Cut to live AI class demo]

A live AI tutor that does not just display content.
It teaches. It asks you questions. It notices when you look
confused and slows down. It notices when you are racing ahead
and speeds up.

It knows your name. It remembers what you studied last week
and what you have not mastered yet.

[Cut to Drive Mode]

It teaches you on your commute — audio only, hands-free —
then picks up exactly where you left off when you sit down again.

[Cut to language selector — 27 flags]

It speaks your language. Twenty-seven of them.
A farmer in rural Cambodia learning accounting in Khmer.
A taxi driver in Nairobi studying programming in Swahili.
An engineer in São Paulo retraining in AI in Portuguese.
The language of your education should not limit what you can learn.

[Cut to Careers dashboard]

And every lesson connects to a real job.
We read current job postings so the curriculum stays current
with what employers actually want today — not three years ago.

[Cut to Kids Mode]

Built safe for children, too. Gamified. Guardrailed. With consent.
A different kind of teacher for a different kind of learner.

[End product demo — return to plain background]

Like Netflix for learning.
Except Netflix just shows you something.
Salareen actually teaches you.

==============================================================
SEGMENT 4 — THE TRUTH [2:15 - 2:35]
Visual: Single white text on dark screen — each line appears alone
Music: Music drops back. Almost silence. Single piano note.
==============================================================

And it does it honestly.

Every answer shows its sources.
Every lesson is grounded in a verified knowledge base.
When it does not know — it says so.

[Pause]

In education, an AI that makes things up is not helpful.
It is dangerous.

We built Salareen to never bluff.

==============================================================
SEGMENT 5 — THE SCALE [2:35 - 2:55]
Visual: The "Power Grid" animation — numbers appearing on screen
Music: Returns — confident, building
==============================================================

And it scales.

Five million learners a day. Ready on day one.
Six point three milliseconds to answer.
A whole campus for the price of one small server.

[Key metric in large type: $0.0012 per learner per month]

The entire platform — live AI classes, Drive Mode,
assessments, adaptive learning, 27 languages —
costs one point two tenths of a cent per learner per month.

That is not a budget. That is a business model.

==============================================================
SEGMENT 6 — THE MARKET [2:55 - 3:10]
Visual: Three concentric rings — TAM, SAM, SOM with dollar figures
Music: Continues — building
==============================================================

The market we are entering is seven trillion dollars per year.

The digitally accessible slice — EdTech — is $340 billion and
growing at 14% annually.

We are entering at the fastest-growing inflection point in the
history of education technology: AI capability has crossed the
threshold of genuine usefulness. Mobile penetration has reached
the developing world. The global teacher shortage has become a
crisis.

These forces converge now. Not in five years. Now.

==============================================================
SEGMENT 7 — WHY US [3:10 - 3:30]
Visual: The team brief — or the platform version history / build
Music: Slightly lighter — we are winning
==============================================================

We did not pitch this idea. We built it.

Salareen version 0.10.0 is a live, deployed, multi-service platform
with a multi-agent AI teaching brain, six microservices,
mobile apps on iOS and Android, and 739 automated tests — all passing.

We have filed a provisional patent on our predictive inference engine.
We are in compliance with FERPA, GDPR, COPPA, and BIPA — by design.

The ask today is not "help us build something."
The ask is "help us scale something that already works."

==============================================================
SEGMENT 8 — THE ASK [3:30 - 4:05]
Visual: Clean use-of-funds breakdown — then milestone timeline
Music: Resolving — warm, steady
==============================================================

We are raising five million dollars.

Two million to acquire learners — at eight dollars per acquisition,
that reaches 250,000 learners. More than enough to prove the model.

One and a half million to advance our AI — fine-tuning our own
education model, extending the Foresight predictive engine,
and preparing the platform for embodied AI tutors.

One million to build the B2B channel — the corporate learning
market that will become our largest revenue stream.

And five hundred thousand for operations and compliance —
the certifications and legal infrastructure that unlock
institutional and government contracts.

At 18 months, we arrive at Series A with proven traction:
real learners, real revenue, real unit economics.

[Pause. Hold. Let it breathe.]

==============================================================
CLOSING [4:05 - 4:30]
Visual: Return to the child in the classroom — then the platform.
Then the tagline.
Music: Returns to the opening piano theme — full circle
==============================================================

[The classroom image from the opening returns]

That child in the front left. The one falling behind.
The one who left school thinking she was not smart.

[Platform appears — the AI tutor begins a class]

She is not in that classroom anymore.

She is learning at her pace.
In her language.
With a teacher that will not give up on her.

[The Bayon Buddy mascot]

This is what we are building.
This is what we are asking you to help us build.

[Final screen — dark background, gold wordmark, tagline]

SALAREEN
Invest in the next 200 years of school.

[Contact information appears]

[MUSIC OUT]

[END]

==============================================================
PRODUCTION NOTES
==============================================================

Voice direction:
- Use a single narrator throughout. Not AI-generated — human voice.
- Warm, slightly accented is fine and authentic to the Khmer/
  multicultural origin of the product name.
- Slow down for statistics. The $0.0012 figure needs a full
  three-second pause before and after.
- The question "What if every learner had their own teacher?"
  should be read at half the speed of the surrounding text.

Music direction:
- Opening: Einaudi-style piano. Single instrument. Sparse.
- Product section: add a subtle percussive layer — not distracting,
  forward-moving. Think Apple product video pacing.
- Scale section: the music builds — this is the "power" moment.
- Closing: return to the piano theme from the opening.
  Full emotional circle.

B-roll to license or capture:
- Real classroom footage (diverse, international — not stock-photo
  American)
- Close-up of a child's face concentrating, then smiling
- Commuter on phone / earbuds (Drive Mode scene)
- Coding on a laptop in a non-Western setting
- Parent and child at a tablet together
- Optional: brief clip of a humanoid robot (public domain or licensed)
  for the "robot-ready" moment in the product section
