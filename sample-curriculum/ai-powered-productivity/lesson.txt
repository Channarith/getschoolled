SLIDE 1 | What This Course Is About
This course helps everyday professionals get real productivity gains from
generative AI assistants such as Microsoft Copilot and Google Gemini, while using
them safely and responsibly. You will learn where these tools genuinely save
time, how to prompt them well, how to keep a human check on their output, and how
to follow your organisation's data rules. No technical background is required, and
every technique here can be applied to your real work this week.
NARRATION: This course gives you practical productivity gains from AI assistants like Copilot and Gemini, used responsibly.

SLIDE 2 | AI Assistants in the Tools You Already Use
Modern AI assistants live inside the apps you use daily: drafting and replying to
email, summarising long documents and meetings, building slide decks, and
analysing spreadsheets. Because they sit where the work already happens, the gain
comes from removing busywork rather than learning a separate tool. The fastest
path to value is to pick one or two high-frequency tasks and build the habit there
before spreading wider.
NARRATION: AI assistants live inside your daily apps; start with one or two frequent tasks to build the habit.

SLIDE 3 | High-Value Use Cases
The biggest time savings come from a handful of patterns: turning rough notes
into a polished draft, summarising a long thread or report into key points,
rewriting text for a different audience or tone, extracting action items from a
meeting, and getting a fast first explanation of an unfamiliar topic. The common
thread is that AI produces a strong first draft quickly, and you refine it with
your judgment.
NARRATION: AI saves the most time on drafts, summaries, rewrites, action items, and quick explanations.

SLIDE 4 | The Anatomy of a Good Prompt
A good prompt gives the assistant four things: a role to adopt, the task to do,
the context it needs, and the format you want back. For example, act as a
customer-support lead, draft a reply to this complaint, the customer is a
long-time client who is frustrated about a delay, and keep it under 120 words and
warm in tone. Specific prompts produce specific, useful results.
NARRATION: Strong prompts specify a role, the task, the context, and the desired output format.

SLIDE 5 | Prompting Techniques That Work
A few habits reliably raise quality. Be explicit about what you want and what to
avoid. Give an example when style matters, which is called few-shot prompting.
Set constraints such as length, reading level, or required sections. For anything
involving reasoning, ask the assistant to work step by step. These additions cost
seconds and consistently improve the output.
NARRATION: Be specific, give examples, set constraints, and ask for step-by-step reasoning.

SLIDE 6 | Iterating Instead of Restarting
The first answer is a starting point, not a verdict. If it misses, tell the
assistant exactly what to change: make it shorter, more formal, more concrete, or
focused on one section. Each round steers the result closer to what you need.
Skilled users expect two or three quick iterations rather than accepting or
discarding the first draft.
NARRATION: Treat prompting as a conversation; refine the draft over a few quick rounds instead of starting over.

SLIDE 7 | Grounding AI in Your Own Material
General assistants do not know your company's private documents, so for accurate,
relevant output you supply the source material, paste the text, attach the file,
or use an enterprise tool that retrieves your documents. Grounding the assistant
in real, trusted content is one of the best ways to get useful answers and to
reduce made-up information.
NARRATION: Give the assistant your own documents as context; grounding it in real sources improves accuracy.

SLIDE 8 | Keeping a Human in Charge
AI assistants can state wrong information fluently and confidently, so you stay
responsible for accuracy, tone, and the final decision. Treat the tool as a fast
assistant whose work you review before it goes out. Verify facts, figures, names,
and quotes against trusted sources, especially for anything you will send,
publish, or act on.
NARRATION: You own accuracy and final decisions; always review and verify AI output before it goes out.

SLIDE 9 | Data Protection and Approved Tools
Never paste confidential, personal, or regulated data into a tool that is not
approved for it, and always follow your organisation's AI and data policy. Prefer
enterprise versions with clear data protections over public consumer tools, and
when in doubt, anonymise the information first. Protecting data is a core part of
using AI professionally.
NARRATION: Follow your data policy, use approved enterprise tools, and never paste sensitive data into public AI.

SLIDE 10 | Responsible and Transparent Use
Responsible use means staying alert to bias in output, being transparent about
where AI was used when honesty matters, and never presenting unchecked AI work as
your own verified output. Where decisions affect people, keep a human firmly in
the loop. Used this way, AI accelerates your work while protecting quality and
trust.
NARRATION: Watch for bias, be transparent about AI use, and keep humans in charge of decisions about people.

SLIDE 11 | Building a Personal AI Workflow
Make the gains stick by building habits: keep a small library of prompts that
work well so you can reuse them, look for repetitive first-draft tasks to
delegate, and share useful techniques with your team. The aim is not to use AI
for everything, but to know exactly when it helps and when human work is the
right call.
NARRATION: Save prompts that work, delegate repetitive drafts, and share techniques to make the gains stick.

SLIDE 12 | Course Summary and Next Steps
You now know where AI assistants save the most time, how to prompt and iterate,
how to ground answers in your own material, and how to use AI responsibly and
securely. Pick one frequent task this week, apply the define-prompt-verify loop,
and build from there. Productivity with AI is a practical habit that grows with
use.
NARRATION: Apply the define, prompt, ground, verify loop to one frequent task this week and build the habit.
