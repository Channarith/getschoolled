SALAREEN — MARKETING RESEARCH BRIEF (sourced)
=============================================
Purpose: defensible, citable statistics for promo videos, investor decks, and
the web site. Every figure below lists its source and the year. Use the exact
phrasing in the "Safe on-screen wording" column when putting a number on screen,
and keep the source visible (small footer line) so claims stay defensible.

HONESTY RULES (read before using any number)
- Cite the source on screen for hard numbers (e.g. "World Bank, 2022").
- Aspirational or anecdotal outcomes ("learned Spanish in 3 days", "remembered
  my course") must carry "Dramatization. Individual results vary."
- Forgetting-curve percentages are research-derived ranges, not a single law —
  word them as "research suggests" / "up to", never as an exact guarantee.
- Don't merge two sources into one invented number. Keep each stat atomic.

A. THE GLOBAL LEARNING CRISIS (use in "Evolution of Learning" + "By the Numbers")
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1) Learning poverty
   FIGURE: ~70% of 10-year-olds in low- and middle-income countries cannot read
           and understand a simple story.
   SOURCE: World Bank / UNESCO, "The State of Global Learning Poverty: 2022
           Update." (post-pandemic estimate; pre-pandemic was ~57%).
   Safe on-screen wording: "70% of 10-year-olds can't read a simple story.
                            — World Bank, 2022"

2) Economic cost of the crisis
   FIGURE: This generation of students risks losing ~$21 trillion in lifetime
           earnings in present value (≈17% of today's global GDP).
   SOURCE: World Bank, UNESCO, UNICEF — "The State of the Global Education
           Crisis: A Path to Recovery" (2021/2022).
   Safe on-screen wording: "$21 trillion in lost lifetime earnings.
                            — World Bank/UNESCO/UNICEF, 2022"

3) Teacher shortage
   FIGURE: The world needs ~44 million additional teachers to reach universal
           basic education by 2030.
   SOURCE: UNESCO / Global Education Monitoring Report & Teacher Task Force,
           2023–2024.
   Safe on-screen wording: "The world is short 44 million teachers.
                            — UNESCO, 2024"

4) Out-of-school children
   FIGURE: ~250+ million children and youth are out of school worldwide
           (UNESCO's 2024 revision put the figure around 251–272M).
   SOURCE: UNESCO, 2024 out-of-school estimates.
   Safe on-screen wording: "250M+ children are out of school. — UNESCO, 2024"

B. RETENTION / FORGETTING (use in "20-Minute Expert" corporate)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5) The forgetting curve
   FIGURE: Without reinforcement, people forget a large majority of newly
           learned information within days; commonly cited as up to ~70% within
           24 hours and ~90% within a week.
   SOURCE: Derived from Hermann Ebbinghaus's forgetting-curve research (1885)
           and modern replications (e.g., Murre & Dros, 2015, PLOS ONE).
   CAUTION: This is a research-derived range, not a fixed constant. Word it as
            "research suggests" or "up to ~90% within a week."
   Safe on-screen wording: "Research suggests we forget up to 90% of new
                            training within a week. — Ebbinghaus; Murre & Dros, 2015"

6) Spaced repetition works
   FIGURE: Spaced/distributed practice reliably improves long-term retention
           vs. massed ("cramming") study — one of the most robust findings in
           learning science.
   SOURCE: Cepeda et al., 2006 (Psychological Bulletin) meta-analysis;
           Dunlosky et al., 2013 (Psychological Science in the Public Interest).
   Safe on-screen wording: "Spaced repetition is proven to make learning stick.
                            — Cepeda et al., 2006"

C. COST / ACCESS (supporting value story)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7) Tutoring is expensive / unequal
   FIGURE: Private 1:1 tutoring in the U.S. commonly runs ~$25–$80+/hour, putting
           high-dose tutoring out of reach for most families; effective
           "high-dosage" tutoring is strongly evidence-backed but costly to staff.
   SOURCE: Industry rate surveys; J-PAL / Education Endowment Foundation reviews
           of high-dosage tutoring effectiveness.
   Safe on-screen wording: "1:1 tutoring: $25–$80/hour — out of reach for most.
                            — industry rates"

8) Chronic absenteeism (U.S.)
   FIGURE: Roughly 1 in 4 U.S. students was chronically absent in recent years
           (post-pandemic peak ~28%).
   SOURCE: U.S. Dept. of Education / NCES; national absenteeism trackers, 2022–2023.
   Safe on-screen wording: "1 in 4 U.S. students is chronically absent. — NCES"
