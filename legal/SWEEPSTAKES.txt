AOEP - REWARDS & SWEEPSTAKES OFFICIAL RULES (TEMPLATE - requires legal review)
=============================================================================
Version: 1.0   Effective: 2026-06-18

The AOEP Rewards program lets learners earn points for course completion and
redeem them for class discounts, items, or entries into prize sweepstakes.

POINTS
- Points are earned for completing courses (more for higher levels, higher
  scores, and hands-on labs). Points have no cash value, are non-transferable,
  and may expire per program terms.

DISCOUNTS & ITEMS
- Points may be redeemed for discount vouchers (a % off a class) or fulfillable
  items while supplies last.

SWEEPSTAKES (high-value prizes, e.g. PlayStation 5, gold bar)
- High-value prizes are awarded by RANDOM DRAWING (sweepstakes), not guaranteed
  to every learner. Spending points buys entries; more entries = better odds.
- NO PURCHASE NECESSARY. A free method of entry is available (request via the
  contact below); a purchase or payment will not improve your chances of winning.
- Void where prohibited. Open only to eligible participants who meet the age and
  jurisdiction requirements; minors may require parent/guardian consent.
- Odds depend on the number of entries. Winners are selected at random, notified,
  and may be required to sign an affidavit/eligibility & liability release.
- PRIZES & TAXES: prizes are awarded "as is"; winners are responsible for all
  applicable taxes. Cash alternatives are at the sponsor's discretion.
- Compliance: the program is operated in accordance with applicable sweepstakes/
  lottery, consumer-protection, and (for minors) child-protection laws of the
  participant's jurisdiction; it is void where such promotions are restricted.

DATA
- Entry/redemption data is handled per the Privacy Notice (legal/PRIVACY.txt).

SPONSOR / CONTACT: rewards@example.org  (placeholder)

NOTE: This is an engineering template. Sweepstakes/lottery law varies widely by
jurisdiction (registration/bonding may be required, e.g. in some US states).
Have qualified counsel finalize official rules before running any prize promotion.
