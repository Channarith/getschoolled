AOEP - DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM (DPA)  (TEMPLATE - requires legal review)
========================================================================
Version: 1.0   Effective: 2026-06-18

This DPA governs processing of personal data / student education records when an
institution ("Controller"/"School") uses AOEP ("Processor"). It supplements the
Terms of Use and applies where FERPA, COPPA, GDPR, or similar laws apply.

1. ROLES
   The School is the controller of student data; AOEP is the processor / "school
   official" acting only on the School's documented instructions.

2. SCOPE & PURPOSE LIMITATION
   AOEP processes student data solely to provide the Service "for the use and
   benefit of the School and for no other commercial purpose" (FTC COPPA school-
   authorization standard). No advertising; no sale; no model training on student
   PII without separate written authorization.

3. COPPA SCHOOL AUTHORIZATION
   Where the School authorizes collection from under-13 students on parents'
   behalf, AOEP provides a description of all personal information collected, how
   it is used, and the ability to review and delete it on request.

4. SUBPROCESSORS
   Listed in NOTICE.txt / on request; bound by equivalent obligations; School is
   notified of changes and may object.

5. SECURITY
   Encryption in transit and at rest for sensitive data (incl. biometric
   embeddings), access controls, signed content/webhooks, and audit logging.

6. DATA SUBJECT / PARENT RIGHTS
   AOEP assists the School with access, correction, deletion, portability, and
   consent-withdrawal requests within statutory timelines.

7. RETENTION & RETURN/DELETION
   Data retained per the School's retention schedule (retention_days), then
   deleted or anonymized; returned/deleted on termination.

8. BREACH NOTIFICATION
   AOEP notifies the School without undue delay (and within GDPR Art. 33
   timelines where applicable) and supports the School's notification duties.

9. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS / DATA RESIDENCY
   Lawful transfer mechanisms; deployments can be pinned to a region/boundary
   (local/edge) to keep data in-jurisdiction.

10. AUDIT
   AOEP provides reasonable information and supports audits to demonstrate
   compliance.
