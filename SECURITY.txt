Expires: 2027-01-01T00:00:00Z

REPORTING A VULNERABILITY
- Email security@example.org with steps to reproduce, impact, and affected
  versions. Encrypt sensitive details if possible.
- Please do NOT open public issues for security reports.
- Do NOT access, modify, or exfiltrate other users' data; never target real
  student/biometric data. Use test accounts and minimal proof-of-concept.

OUR COMMITMENT (coordinated disclosure)
- Acknowledge your report within 3 business days.
- Provide a remediation timeline and keep you updated.
- Credit reporters who wish to be named after a fix ships.
- Safe harbor: good-faith research that follows this policy will not be pursued
  legally and is authorized for the purposes of CFAA-type statutes.

SCOPE
- The AOEP services (orchestrator, curriculum, memory, speech, perception,
  billing, integrations, harvester), the web app, and the shared library.
- Out of scope: third-party services, social engineering, physical attacks,
  and volumetric DoS.

SECURITY-RELEVANT FEATURES IN THIS REPO
- Tamper-evident content credentials / provenance (HMAC-signed) - see
  packages/shared/src/aoep_shared/provenance.py and the /verify page.
- Webhook signature verification (inbound + outbound) - aoep_shared/webhooks.py.
- Consent-gated biometrics; encrypted, deletable face embeddings (db migrations).
- Hallucination guard + human-in-the-loop review for high-risk output.

HANDLING SECURITY NOTIFICATIONS TO USERS
- Material security incidents affecting personal/student data are disclosed to
  affected institutions/users per applicable breach-notification laws (e.g. GDPR
  Art. 33/34, US state breach laws) and the timelines in legal/PRIVACY.txt.
